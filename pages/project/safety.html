Safety

Strain Stability and Biosafety

Pseudomonas putida is a well characterized, non-pathogenic soil bacterium with extensive research and industry applications biotechnology. Genetic modifications done in this project are all chromosomal and markerless guaranteeing long-term stability (>50 generations) reducing the possibility of horizontal gene transfer. Biological containment is maintained in normal laboratory conditions and without releasing anything to the environment.

Dual use research of concern (DURC)

The organisms and parts that we will be working with are in Risk Group 1. They are non-pathogenic and non-spore forming and all our work is covered by the White List, hence, we will not be conducting any activities against the guidelines provided by the competition.

Dual Use in the context of science describes the potential of knowledge or technologies to be used by third parties with benevolent and malevolent intentions. (European Commission, 2018)

Dual-use research of concern (DURC) describes research that is intended to provide a clear benefit, but which could easily be misapplied to do harm. It usually refers to work in the life sciences, but the principles apply to other fields including engineering and information technology. (World Health Organisation, 2020)

Our project involves blocking a metabolic step forcing accumulation of an intermediate leading to potential misuse risks. Someone could generalize the idea to engineer other microbes to overproduce harmful and toxic intermediates. While our work is intended solely for producing a commonly existing product sustainably, we acknowledge the importance of implementing safeguards. This includes responsible biosafety to minimize the risk of misuse.
