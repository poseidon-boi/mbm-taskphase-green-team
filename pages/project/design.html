Design

Homologous Arm Selection

To achieve a precise knockout of the cymC gene via homologous recombination, two genomic regions flanking the target locus were selected as homologous arms. An 800 bp upstream homologous arm (UHA) was chosen from the region immediately upstream of the cymC coding sequence, and an 800 bp downstream homologous arm (DHA) was selected from the region immediately downstream.

Homology arms of approximately 800 bp (Protocol for Constructing a Gene Knockout in Pseudomonas Using pT18mobsacB) were selected to maximize recombination efficiency while minimizing unintended recombination events elsewhere in the genome.

The cymC coding sequence itself was deliberately excluded from the construct design to ensure complete removal of the gene following allelic exchange.

Fig 1. P. putida F1 genome sequence viewer

Insert DNA Architecture

The insert DNA was designed as a continuous UHA–DHA fusion, forming a deletion cassette that replaces the native cymC locus upon double-crossover recombination. This architecture enables seamless excision of the chromosomal cymC gene without leaving behind antibiotic resistance markers or foreign genetic elements.

By relying exclusively on native genomic sequences for recombination, this design ensures a clean and stable knockout, which is particularly important for downstream metabolic analysis and long-term strain stability.

XmaI and PstI restriction sites were added to the upstream and downstream ends, respectively, to facilitate restriction cloning and insertion of the construct into the plasmid, following established protocols (Yang et al., 2025).

Fig 2. Insertion of fragment into pT18mobsacB

Primer Design Considerations

Primers were designed to amplify the homologous arms from genomic DNA with high specificity and efficiency. Both primers were 25 bp in length, providing sufficient binding specificity, with melting temperatures of 68°C for the UHA forward primer and 68.5°C for the DHA reverse primer. The GC content of both primers was maintained at 60% to promote stable annealing and uniform amplification conditions. (Protocol for Constructing a Gene Knockout in Pseudomonas Using pT18mobsacB)

In Silico Plasmid Construction

The complete suicide plasmid carrying the UHA–DHA insert was constructed in silico using SnapGene. The backbone plasmid was virtually digested using XmaI and PstI, and the designed insert DNA was ligated into the multiple cloning site using restriction cloning. The final construct was validated in silico by confirming correct insert orientation, intact recombination junctions, and absence of unintended sequence alterations. This computational validation step ensured that the construct was cloning-ready before proceeding to wet-lab implementation.
