Geethika Mopidevi

  • Coir Pith Project: Contributed to project ideation and researched polymer coatings (xanthan gum, PGA) and embedded chitosan nanoparticles. Evaluated key physical properties of coir pith, including water retention capacity, and assessed environmental impact through comparison with silica beads.
  • Cosmic Cleanse: Developed the concept and investigated enzymatic systems (lipases, proteases), enzyme stability, and design constraints under microgravity conditions. Explored peptide-infused fabric technologies and proposed a multilayer patch or clothing system using a cell-free approach.
  • Perfume Project – γ-Decalactone: Investigated plausible enzyme replacements within the biosynthetic pathway and contributed to the of Integrated Human Practices (IHP) questions.
  • Perfume Project – p-Cumene/Cuminaldehyde: Studied aldehyde toxicity, retention in biological systems, and detailed metabolic pathways, including key enzymes, genes, and microbial strains. Worked on whole-cell immobilization, inverted emulsion extraction strategies, stakeholder outreach (HP/IHP), and development of an education plan.
  • Additional Projects: Analyzed existing control strategies and invasion mechanisms related to the rhinoceros beetle, and researched synthetic biology approaches for heme production with emphasis on improving absorption and bioavailability.

Trayee Sridharan

  • Coir Pith Project: Contributed to ideation and early conceptual development. Researched polymer coatings such as PGA and xanthan gum and evaluated their interactions with coir pith, along with assessing the functional impact of pith within the proposed system.

  • Other Projects:Contributed to preliminary research and solution ideation for projects later discontinued, including reviewing existing methods for the beetles project and ideating solutions for the lactose-related problem.
