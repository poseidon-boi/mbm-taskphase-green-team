Whole-Genome BLAST Analysis Against the Target Aldehyde Gene

We performed a whole-genome BLAST analysis of Pseudomonas putida F1 against our target aldehyde-related gene (cymC) to identify homologous sequences and confirm the presence.

Before designing a gene knockout strategy, it was essential to:

  • Confirm that cymC is present as a single, well-defined locus in the genome
  • Check for paralogous or homologous genes that could compensate for cymC deletion
  • Ensure that targeting cymC would not unintentionally affect other aldehyde-metabolising enzymes
  • Verify that the gene sequence used for the Upstream Homologous Arm and the Downstream Homologous Arm design is accurate

A BLASTn search was performed using the Pseudomonas putida F1 genome as the subject.

Inference : BLAST analysis showed a perfect match between the query gene and the Pseudomonas putida F1 genome, with 100% identity and an E-value of 0, indicating a highly significant alignment.

Aldehyde Dehydrogenase Specificity Analysis

We performed a comparative BLAST analysis to evaluate whether other aldehyde dehydrogenase enzymes present in Pseudomonas putida F1 could potentially interfere with or compensate for the function of our target aldehyde-related gene (cymC).A. putida contains multiple aldehyde dehydrogenases involved in diverse metabolic pathways.
Before proceeding with a gene knockout strategy, it was important to assess:

  • Potential functional redundancy
  • Possible off-target aldehyde metabolism
  • The likelihood of other enzymes compensating for the loss of cymC

Reviewed aldehyde dehydrogenase proteins from P. putida F1 were retrieved from UniProt (Swiss-Prot). A total of six aldehyde dehydrogenase candidates were identified. The protein sequences were individually subjected to BLAST analysis with CymC.

The BLAST results show that while P. putida has other aldehyde dehydrogenases, the closest hits (betB and astD) have only moderate to low sequence similarity to cymC. This suggests they are unlikely to compensate for its function, confirming cymC as a specific and suitable knockout target.

Identification of Upstream and Downstream Homology Arms

We identified the regions flanking the target gene (cymC) in the P. putida F1 genome to design the upstream and downstream homology arms (UHA and DHA) required for homologous recombination.

Using the BLAST results from the genome vs. cymC gene, we determined the exact genomic coordinates of the target gene.

The orientation (plus/minus strand) was noted to correctly identify upstream and downstream regions.

Sequences upstream (UHA) and downstream (DHA) of the gene were extracted. Reverse complement sequences were converted to 5′ → 3′ orientation to prepare them for primer design and cloning.

Construction of the Insert DNA

UHA and DHA sequences were joined in silico to form a continuous segment.

Restriction sites for XmaI and PstI were added at the ends to allow the insert to ligate with the plasmid backbone.

Primers were designed specific to this segment with the following parameters:

  • Length: ~35 nucleotides
  • GC content: 50–60%
  • Melting temperature (Tm)
