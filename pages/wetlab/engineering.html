Knocking out cymC

Aim :
To enhance accumulation of the fragrance-relevant intermediate p-cumic aldehyde in Pseudomonas putida F1 by constructing a precise chromosomal knockout of cymC gene responsible for encoding p-cumic aldehyde dehydrogenase via homologous recombination, thereby blocking the conversion of p-cumic aldehyde to p-cumate and functionally arresting the p-cymene oxidation module at the aldehyde stage.​

Fig: cym and cmt operon of Pseudomonas putida F1 strain

Mechanism of the plasmid

The suicide plasmid - a non-replicative Pseudomonas vector called pT18mobsacB was created for precise genome editing through homologous recombination. The target gene's upstream and downstream homologous arms force chromosomal integration due to its incapacity to replicate independently, allowing for single- and double-crossover events for targeted deletion.To isolate cells that have experienced allelic exchange, the plasmid contains the sacB gene for negative counter-selection and a tetracycline resistance marker for positive selection of integrants. Targeted recombination, selectable markers, and counter-selection work together to make pT18mobsacB a powerful tool for controlled genomic modification and site-specific gene knockout in non-model hosts.

Fig: Plasmid design

Chromosomal Integration and Homologous Recombination

The verified knockout plasmid pT18mobsacB-UHA-DHA was introduced into Pseudomonas cells by heat shock.Because pT18mobsacB is a suicide plasmid that cannot replicate in Pseudomonas, integration of the plasmid into the chromosome through homologous recombination is necessary for survival under tetracycline selection. A single-crossover integrant (merodiploid) with the complete plasmid sequence inserted into the genome is produced by this initial recombination event between the upstream or downstream homologous arm and the corresponding chromosomal region. Tetracycline resistance and the retention of the sacB gene allow for subsequent counter-selection in these integrants.Correct chromosomal integration was verified by colony PCR using primers spanning the plasmid chromosome junctions, confirming successful single-crossover recombination

​​To obtain a true gene knockout, confirmed single-crossover Pseudomonas integrants were grown without antibiotic selection and plated on sucrose-containing medium. The sacB gene on pT18mobsacB is lethal in Gram-negative bacteria in the presence of sucrose, so only cells that have lost the plasmid through a second homologous recombination event can survive. This double crossover results in either restoration of the wild-type allele or precise deletion of the target gene. Sucrose-resistant colonies were screened for tetracycline sensitivity and confirmed using primers flanking the target locus in diagnostic PCR. Successful knockout strains, which showed a smaller fragment size corresponding to the joined homologous arms and lack of the target gene, verified a clean, marker-free deletion.

Extraction

To optimize the production of p-cuminaldehyde and to increase the yield,we have employed a whole cell encapsulation strategy for our extraction and downstream processing involving a multi-compartmentalised emulsion in an organic solvent system and a bisulfite extraction protocol for increased recovery of our final product from the cell - cuminaldehyde. This was chosen as it protects the intracellular enzymes from harsh conditions like the organic solvent and for higher yields.(Zhao et al., 2018)

Proposed DBTL Cycle

Design

Target Identification

  • Selected Pseudomonas putida F1 as the production chassis and cymC as the knockout target to terminate the p-cymene catabolic pathway at the aldehyde stage.
  • Performed whole-genome BLAST analysis to confirm that cymC is a single-copy locus and screened for homologous enzymes that could compensate for its deletion.

Genetic Design

  • Designed upstream (UHA) and downstream (DHA) homology arms flanking the cymC locus.
  • Selected the suicide vector pT18mobsacB for homologous recombination and designed primers compatible with XmaI and PstI restriction sites for plasmid assembly.

System Design

  • Designed a whole-cell encapsulation strategy using the PCl21-PEG45-PCl21 triblock polymer to protect cells from organic solvents and the toxic intermediate p-cuminaldehyde during extraction.

Build

Plasmid Construction

  • Digested the pT18mobsacB vector with XmaI and PstI to linearise the backbone.
  • Amplified UHA (0.8–1.0 kb) and DHA (0.5–0.8 kb) fragments from P. putida genomic DNA using high-fidelity PCR.
  • Assembled the knockout plasmid (pT18-UHA-DHA).
  • Transformed the assembled plasmid into Escherichia coli DH5α for propagation.

Strain Engineering

  • Introduced the suicide plasmid into Pseudomonas putida F1.
  • First recombination: selected tetracycline-resistant colonies representing single-crossover integrants.
  • Second recombination: performed sucrose counter-selection to force plasmid excision via double crossover, resulting in deletion of cymC.

Encapsulation System Setup

  • Immobilised engineered cells within the triblock polymer and established the organic and aqueous phases required for the biphasic extraction system.

Test

Genotypic Validation

  • Confirmed the cymC knockout using colony PCR with primers flanking the deletion site.

Phenotypic Characterisation

  • Performed confirmatory assays including Gram staining, indole test, and MR–VP tests to verify strain identity and culture purity.
  • Determined the minimum inhibitory concentration (MIC) of p-cuminaldehyde to evaluate toxicity limits.

Production and Extraction

  • Induced p-cuminaldehyde production by adding p-cymene to the encapsulated whole-cell system.
  • Isolated the aldehyde from the organic phase using a bisulphite extraction protocol.

Quantification

  • Developed a reverse-phase HPLC method for product analysis.
  • Generated a calibration curve to quantify p-cuminaldehyde yields from the fermentation system.

Learn

Model Validation

  • Compared experimentally determined HPLC yields with theoretical production predictions to assess system performance.

Human Practices Integration

  • Incorporated feedback from domain experts and stakeholders to refine the next iteration of the production chassis and extraction strategy.
