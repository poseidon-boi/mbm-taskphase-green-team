Measurements

Qualitative analysis

  • Note down the time at which the peak appears when running standard stock solutions of known concentrations.
  • If the peak from the extracted p-cuminaldehyde appears at the same time, then we can confirm that our cells have p-cuminaldehyde.
  • If peaks occur at other times, then we can confirm that some other products were formed.

Quantitative analysis

The machine measures the area under the curve for each standard solution of known concentration, plots it, and generates a straight-line equation:

y = mx + c

  • y = peak area
  • x = concentration (ppm or mg/L)
  • m = sensitivity of the detector
  • c = background noise

When the bacterial extract samples are run, the machine detects their peak and integrates it, providing the area under the curve. This value is substituted into the equation to calculate the concentration of p-cuminaldehyde in the vial.

The calculated concentration is multiplied by the volume of liquid in the vial (in litres), and then divided by the volume of culture used.

Example Calculation

Given:

  • Measured concentration (x) = 30 mg/L
  • Volume of liquid in vial = 1 mL = 0.001 L
  • Volume of culture used = 50 mL = 0.05 L

Amount of compound in vial: 30 mg/L × 0.001 L = 0.03 mg

Yield per litre of culture: 0.03 mg ÷ 0.05 L = 0.6 mg/L

The total yield from the culture is 0.6 mg/L.

Limit of Detection and Limit of Quantification

The limit of detection (LOD) and the limit of quantification (LOQ) of p-cuminaldehyde are derived using the following equations as per ICH guidelines.

LOD = 3.3 × σ / S
LOQ = 10 × σ / S

Where,

  • σ = standard deviation of the y-intercept
  • S = mean slope of the calibration curve
