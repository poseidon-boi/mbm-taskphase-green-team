Integrated Human Practices

Dr. Jonathan Müller

Date- 10/1/26
We reached out to Dr. Jonathan Muller to discuss his thesis on the synthesis of γ-decalactone and other terpenoids. Through this interaction we gained quite a few valuable insights into potential strategies for improving yield by identifying the bottlenecks he encountered. We also learned about specific enzyme mutations in BM3 and thioesterase and explored whether modifying these enzymes or employing a different chassis could enhance production. Additionally, he shared some of the practical challenges he faced during his experiments, which would have helped us design our own approach had we picked this project.

Dr. Mohammed Rasoul Sammandari

Date- 12-11-16
We reached out to him to understand the lab protocols described in his paper, which were detailed and informative. This also gave us valuable insights into the heterologous pathway, further deepening our understanding of how the enzymes function.This was also done for one of the previous projects.

Dr. Zijay Tang

As a part of our Human Practices, we reached out to Dr. Zijay Tang for one of our previous projects to gain insight to the practical limitations of the DEPCOS system for environmental sensing. He highlighted that the maintenance of cellular activity with continuous nutrient availability and proper temperature was the main factor that limited its lifetime. He also explained that alginate-based shells need a constant supply of ions like calcium to stay stable and avoid swelling. These insights helped us better understand the trade-offs between biosafety, material stability, and long-term deployment.
